ACMx is not only the Colorado School of Mines student branch of the Association for Computing Machinery, but also a project-oriented group dedicated to improving student life through technology.

Becoming an ACMx member is free, and meetings are held weekly on campus when school is in session. Meetings typically last around 90 minutes, and are used to plan group events, give project status updates, and for project breakout time. Groups often meet individually for longer periods to just work on their projects.

Projects are student-initiated and student-led, funded by the ACMx group budget and donations. To join an ACMx project group, or to propose your own project idea, come to a meeting!
